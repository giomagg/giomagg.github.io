Research

My research is focused on understanding how access to (which) information – and the technologies we use to do so – shapes individuals’ relations with themselves and the collective. Mainly influenced by the philosophy of Foucault, Latour, and Deleuze and Guattari, I work on modelling how micro-dynamics of interactions come to structure the (online) social and public space – with a special focus on studying opinion dynamics, disinformation, and discursive polarization. I tackle these questions using methodologies from the computational social sciences – especially data collection using web scraping and data analysis using network and text analysis (word embeddings, LLMs).
